⚡ Trip Tips & Safety Notes

CategoryTip
Angel's LandingStart by 9 AM per your permit. Chain section is exposed with 1,000+ ft drops on both sides. Grippy gloves help. Let faster hikers pass. Don't rush. If icy or windy, turn back — use judgment.
Delicate Arch3 mi RT, ~1.5–2 hrs. No shade, exposed sandstone. Bring 1L water minimum. Morning is cooler. Final bowl approach is a sloping ledge — stay aware of footing.
Bryce Navajo LoopSteep switchbacks descending 550 ft. Can be icy in early March — ice cleats recommended. The Wall Street section is narrow and shaded. Yaktrax also essential for rim viewpoints (Sunrise/Sunset Point paved but icy).
The NarrowsMarch water temps in the low 40s °F. Waterproof boots + neoprene socks strongly recommended. Check river flow at Zion visitor center — above 150 cfs gets sporty. Rental gear at Zion Outfitter, Springdale.
Ankle: FootwearLace-up ankle brace or supportive hiking boot is non-negotiable. Even 'flat' Utah trails have uneven sandy/rocky patches.
Ankle: Bryce ColdBryce sits at ~8,000 ft. Cold + altitude makes a sprained ankle stiffer and more painful, especially in the AM. Budget extra warm-up time.
Ankle: Dead HorseParking lot to overlook is short but uneven terrain. Bring the folding stool — standing on rocky ground waiting for sunset is tiring.
Ankle: Day 4 Driving350–420 miles with a sprained ankle for 6+ hrs is genuinely fatiguing. Use cruise control aggressively on open highway.
Ankle: No Zero Day9 consecutive days with no built-in rest day. If ankle flares on Day 3 or 4, flag Days 3 or 6 as potential full-rest days.
Medical SuppliesPack: KT tape / athletic tape, Voltaren gel (nightly), 2 instant cold packs in car (no ice at trailheads).
Rental CarConfirm HIGH-CLEARANCE SUV at SLC. Check rental agreement for unpaved road coverage — many contracts exclude dirt road damage. Moonscape/Bentonite Hills on Day 4 needs high clearance.
Fuel StrategyFuel up the night before each big drive day: D1 evening (D2 Arches) · D3 evening (D4 Hanksville) · D7 evening (D8 early Zion) · D8 evening (D9 Vegas).
America the BeautifulBuy at Arches entrance Day 2. Covers Arches, Bryce, Zion NPS. Does NOT cover Utah State Parks (Goblin Valley, Kodachrome, Dead Horse, Snow Canyon).
WaterCarry minimum 2L per person per day. Utah is DRY. Dehydration worsens ankle swelling and fatigue on hikes.
Snow CanyonPetrified Dunes slickrock involves uneven terrain — grippy footwear essential. Jenny's Canyon is a short slot canyon, no technical skills needed.
Nearest Urgent CareMoab: Moab Regional Hospital (450 W Williams Way). Springdale: Intermountain St. George (~45 min drive).
